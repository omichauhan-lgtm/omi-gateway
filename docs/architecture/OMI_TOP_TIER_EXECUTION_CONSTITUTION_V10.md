# OMI Top-Tier Execution Constitution V10

This document defines the governing execution principles of the OMI Gateway. It prioritizes evidence, adoption, distribution, and trust over architectural complexity to maximize open-source credibility, government positioning, and enterprise readiness.

---

## Meta
- **Classification:** Sovereign Cognitive Reliability Infrastructure
- **Maturity Stage:** External Validation & Ecosystem Expansion
- **North Star:** Become the reliability layer for AI systems

---

## Strategic Truths

> [!IMPORTANT]
> The largest risk is no longer technical failure.
> The largest risk is building an extraordinary system that nobody uses.

---

## Primary Objective

The primary objective of OMI is to transform from an internally validated research system into an externally trusted, measurable, adopted, and funded reliability ecosystem.

## Core Tracks

### Track A: Product & Adoption
*Objective: Acquire real users and real telemetry.*

- **Phase G3 Targets:**
  - Users: 100
  - Requests: 10,000
  - Active Projects: 10
  - *Mandatory Features:* Public API, Interactive Dashboard, Onboarding Docs, Usage Analytics.
  
- **Phase G4 Targets:**
  - Users: 1,000
  - Requests: 100,000
  - *Mandatory Features:* Self-Hosting Guide, Docker Support, Public Metrics.

### Track B: Scientific Credibility
*Objective: Become the most measurable reliability platform.*

- **Mandatory Publications:**
  - Monthly Reliability Report
  - Monthly Calibration Report
  - Monthly Drift Report
  - Monthly Sovereign AI Report
  - Annual Reliability Benchmark
- **Scientific Requirements:**
  - Mandatory Reproducibility, Confidence Intervals, p-values, Public Datasets, and External Reproduction.

### Track C: Data Moat
*Objective: Accumulate unique operational intelligence.*

- **Moat Data Telemetry:**
  - Provider drift history, Hallucination topology, Calibration evolution, Escalation economics, Indic reliability metrics, and Multilingual failure patterns.
- *Strategic Truth:* Models become commodities. Reliability telemetry becomes a moat.

### Track D: IndiaAI Alignment
*Objective: Align with national AI infrastructure.*

- **Positioning:** Public AI Reliability Infrastructure
- **We are:** Reliability Infrastructure, Sovereign Orchestration, AI Governance Layer, Public Digital Infrastructure.
- **We are NOT:** A chatbot, agent startup, or AGI platform.
- **Government Targets:** IndiaAI Mission, MeitY, Digital Public Infrastructure, State Government AI Programs.

### Track E: Enterprise Readiness
*Objective: Prepare for enterprise pilots.*

- **Mandatory Features:** Audit logs, Observability, Role-based access (RBAC), Governance reports, and Deployment guides.
- **Enterprise Metrics:** Measurable reliability improvements, cost savings, and governance compliance.

### Track F: Open Source Growth
*Objective: Become an ecosystem, not a repository.*

- **Required Assets:** Mature CONTRIBUTING.md, Good First Issues, Public Benchmark Suite, Public Roadmap, Community Discussions.
- **Growth Targets:**
  - GitHub Stars: 100 (Goal 1), 1,000 (Goal 2)
  - Contributors: 10 (Goal 1), 50 (Goal 2)

## Anti-Overengineering Rules

1. **Principle 1:** No new governance layer without external demand.
2. **Principle 2:** No new complexity without measurable value.
3. **Principle 3:** No scaling work without real traffic.
4. **Principle 4:** No architecture work before adoption work.

> [!WARNING]
> The most likely failure mode is architectural excellence combined with market irrelevance.
