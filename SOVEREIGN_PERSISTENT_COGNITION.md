# Sovereign Persistent Cognition: Architectural Design for Public AI Infrastructure

**Operational Phase:** Adaptive Cognitive Ecosystem Equilibrium (Phase 38)
**Classification:** Persistent Cognitive Systems Research

## Abstract
National AI infrastructures require high-integrity, secure, and verifiable cognitive assets. This architectural blueprint outlines OMI's sovereign persistent cognition framework. We describe the design patterns for caching, recursive limits, and immune defenses that prevent systemic collapse under adversarial and organic noise.

## Core Architectural Pillars

### 1. Bounded Recursion Limits
Runaway self-referential expansion degrades latency and exhausts compute budgets. We enforce:
* **Maximum Recursive Governance Depth:** $\le 3$
* **Maximum Meta-Governance Layers:** $\le 2$
* **Maximum Ecosystem Dependency Depth:** $\le 5$

### 2. Cognitive Immune Defense
We treat semantic cache entries as cells in a biological organism. The immune system flags anomalies (e.g. low utility score or elevated drift) and recommend quarantine actions:
* **Detection:** Real-time distance checking of cache embeddings.
* **Isolation:** Quarantining entries associated with failed tasks.
* **Healing:** Provenance pruning to trim old ancestry, utility decay to prevent cache stagnation, and controlled revalidation to re-introduce diversity.

### 3. Public Sandbox and Alignment
A resilient public copilot or citizen portal (CPGRAMS, Jan Sahayak) demands robust protection against injection and poisoning attempts. The immune system ensures that coordinated contamination cascades are isolated immediately, maintaining an adversarial containment rate of $\ge 80\%$.

## Conclusion
Persistent sovereign cognition bridges the gap between raw generative models and auditable public service infrastructure. By treating the routing system as a governed adaptive ecosystem, we build resilience directly into the intelligence substrate.
